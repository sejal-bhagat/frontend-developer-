Why are Lab tests essential?
Lab tests or diagnostic tests are recommended by doctors for diagnosis, screening and monitoring of certain health conditions. Samples of your blood, stool, urine, or mucus can reveal a lot about the state of your health when analysed with special devices. Once the problem is detected, your doctor can diagnose it and prescribe medication or recommend a course of treatment.
How long does it take for the report to be delivered?
It takes 12 to 24 hours for the lab to prepare your report and deliver it to you. However, this time may vary, as the exact turnaround depends on the location of the sample collection.
How should one prepare for a lab test?
Different tests have different requirements. Some tests may require fasting, whereas others may require consuming food before the sample is collected. Consult with your doctor to determine the necessary preparations for a lab test. Please inform your doctor if you are currently taking any medications.
How are lab test reports interpreted?
The reports that our labs will deliver to you will be quite detailed. There will be charts of reference ranges, and our swasth AI feature can help you understand the report. But, once you receive your report, it is best to consult your doctor and ask him/her to explain it to you.
If the lab test results are within normal range, does that mean no illness has been detected?
If your reports are within the normal range, it is certainly good news. But normal results do not always give you a clean bill of health. If symptoms persist, your doctor will recommend other tests to detect what is triggering the unusual signs.
